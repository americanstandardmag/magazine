About the American Standard

This non-establishment left magazine is a passion project organized by Trae Hall, Andrew Klinke, Grant Morgan, Nicholas Trout, and our many guest authors. Keep an eye out for our monthly magazines! Website created and maintained by Madalynn McErlean.

About the team

Hello, my name is Traejen "Trae" Hall. I grew up in the quiet town of Chariton, Iowa, and my childhood gave me an appreciation for many things: one’s labor, the land, our natural world, what it means to be in a community. My politics could broadly be described as left-of-center, and I believe that we are at our best when we work together. I have an associate’s degree in liberal arts from Des Moines Area Community College and a bachelor’s degree in American politics and minors in environmental policy and history. Much of my professional career thus far has been in electoral politics and state government, working as a campaign manager for three candidates and a legislative assistant for a state senator. I look forward to writing with my good friends and colleagues here at the American Standard.

Hi! I am Andrew Klinke. I live in Madison, Wisconsin, better known as a liberal theme park surrounded by a sea of Midwestern reality. I have two Bachelor degrees from Drake University one in International Relations and the other in International Business. I am most passionate about Transit and Urban planning, however I will write the occasional piece on foreign relations. I have a borderline autistic level of knowledge of all things to do with Train that I am excited to bring to the American Standard!

My name is Grant Morgan and I am a staff writer at the American Standard who is based in Washington, DC. I originally grew up in Southern Illinois and attended Drake University in Des Moines, Iowa for my BA in political science. I then, like a fool, proceeded to get my MA in international affairs during a moment of unprecedented global change. When I’m not thinking about history books or fun little musical parodies, I am likely networking and exploring all the wonderful spots in DC I can find. My writing interests are mainly focused on US foreign policy and international economics, but I like to explore and read about a little bit of everything. If you want to chat or have an idea for a pitch, feel free to reach me at gmorgan2001@gmail.com.

My name is Nicholas Trout. I live in Silver Spring, Maryland after spending most of my life in Iowa. I have a Bachelor’s degree from Drake University in Politics and International Relations and a Master’s degree in Security Policy Studies from George Washington University. My interests are US foreign policy, election analysis, and local politics. I hope that my experience living in the Heartland and inside the Beltway gives me a unique perspective on the key issues facing our country, and I am excited to bring my perspective to the American Standard.

Heya! I'm Madalynn McErlean, and I'm the website creator and tech wizard for this magazine. With Bachelor degrees in Computer Science and Data Analytics from Drake University, I'm in charge of the website creation, maintenance, and analytics. While I may not contribute much in writing, I make up for it with my coding, analysis, and designs for this magazine.
